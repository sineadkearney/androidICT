Flash Browsers

While I used to be able to 'side-load' the legacy Adobe Flash Player (.apk) file, this does not seem to be so easily done with Android 4.4.2 onwards. Below, I have trawled through a number of alternative Browsers to see how I can continue to access educational Flash content


Tested on Lenovo Yoga Tablet 2, Android 4.4.2 (and also on Samsung Note, Android 4.4.2) and using these two 'flash' websites

(1) Drag and Drop: scholastic (the 'Drag and Drop: ' below')

(2) Flash Video: mystudymate.ie (the 'Flash Video' below)

(both sites work perfectly on my Yarvik Tablet with Android 4.1.1)


Photon

Link to Google Play

Ad supported; upgrade for Euro7.96.

This is 'Flash in the Cloud'! Screen has icons for Touch/Mouse/Drag (a toggle) and Keyboard.

Drag and Drop: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content ..but click on the 'Lightening Bolt' enables the Flash. Click objects to hear sounds; click and hold for a second or two and then do the Dragging

Flash Video: click on the Lightening Bolt and flash video then plays


FlashFox

Link to Google Play

Free version is ad supported, Euro2.49 to upgrade

Doesn't support Nvideo Tegra nor Intel processors, but on other devices...

Drag and Drop: Loads the Flash site, makes the Sounds when clicked, does not do the 'Drag and Drop' part

Flash video: does play the flash video files


Dolphin Browser

Link to Google Play

You also need the 'Dolphin Jetpack' from jetpack

...and you will the Adobe Flash .apk - follow the Download link at flash.apk

Drag and Drop: Lenovo: 'missing plug-in'. Samsung: Loads the Flash site, makes the Sounds when clicked, does not do the 'Drag and Drop' part

Flash video: Lenovo: 'missing plug-in'. Samsung: does play the flash video files


Puffin (Free)

Link to Google Play

Free to use for two weeks. Ad Supported

Drag and Drop: Loads the Flash site, makes the Sounds when clicked, does not do the 'Drag and Drop' part

Flash Video: does play the flash video files


Chrome Browser

Link to Google Play

Drag and Drop: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content: just a message saying 'the plug-in is not supported'

Flash Video: does play the flash video files (you are redirected to the YouTube app!)


Opera

Link to Google Play

Drag and Drop: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content

Flash Video: does not play the flash video files


Now Browser

Link to Google Play

Drag and Drop: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content

Flash Video: does not play the flash video files


Boat

Link to Google Play

This is the free version.

Uses 'ask' as its default search engine

Drag and Drop: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content: just a message saying 'couldn't load plug-in'

Flash video: does not play the flash video files


Lightening Browser

Link to Google Play

Drag and Drop: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content

Flash video: does not play the flash video files


Firefox

Link to Google Play

Drag and Drop: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content: just a message saying 'a plug-in is needed to display the content'

Flash video: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content: just a message saying 'a plug-in is needed to display the content'


UC Browser for Android

Comes pre-installed (as well as the default Chrome) on Lenovo Yoga Tablets

Link to Google Play

Drag and Drop: Does not load (completely) the Flash site ...one can't see the Flash content

Flash video: does not play the flash video files


Skyfire

Download the .apk file from Softonic at softonic

Seems to need unnecessary access to your tablet (web bookmarks and history; contents of SD card; set wallpaper; modify system settings) so I didn't install
